Ranking the Best Goaltenders of All Time

Sure- rankings are all subjective. However there are few greats in the game that everyone can agree have shaped the league and the way goaltenders play today. Here we will examine some of the best goaltenders from each generation.

1980's

Bob Froese

Bob Froese is a Canadian former professional goaltender who played eight seasons in the (NHL) for the Philadelphia Flyers and the New York Rangers. In 1986, Froese made the NHL All-Star team and won the William H. Jennings award for the least goals allowed in a single season. Froese took his teams to the playoffs 5 out of the 8 years he participated in the NHL.

Ron Hextall

Ron Hextall is a former professional net minder who played 13 season in the NHL for the Philadelphia Flyers, Quebec Nordiques, and the New York Islanders. Following his playing career, Hextall spent time as the General Manager of the Flyers and an Assistant General Manager for the Los Angeles Kings. In his rookie season in 1987, Hextall made the NHL All-Star team, and won both the Vezina trophy for the top goaltender and the Conn Smythe for MVP of the playoffs after leading his team to the Stanley Cup finals.

1990's

Dominik Hasek

Dominik Hasek is Czech former goaltender who played 16 seasons in the NHL for the Chicago Blackhawks, Buffalo Sabres, Detroit Red Wings, and the Ottawa Senators. Nicknamed “The Dominator,” Hasek won six Vezina Trophies for the best goalie- the most ever awarded to a single player. Hasek won the Stanley Cup with the Detroit Red Wings in both 2002 and 2008. Hasek was a two-time winner of the Hart Memorial MVP trophy, three-time Jennings trophy winner, two-time Ted Lindsay best player by the NHLPA, and six-time all star. Hasek was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 2014.

Patrick Roy

Patrick Roy is a former NHL goaltender who spent 20 years in the NHL with the Montreal Canadiens and Colorado Avalanche. Roy took home the Stanley Cup four times during his career- in 1986 and 1993 with the Canadiens, and 1996 and 2001 with the Avalanche. Roy was a five-time Ted Lindsay award winner, three-time Conn Smythe MVP of the playoffs, three-time Vezina best goalie award winner, and a six-time all star. Roy was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 2006.

Roberto Luongo

Roberto Luongo is a Canadian goaltender who has spent 19 years in the NHL with the New York Islanders, Vancouver Canucks, and the Florida Panthers. Luongo has led his teams to the playoffs 7 times. Luongo is a two-time all star and the winner of the Jennings trophy in 2011.

Active Players

Marc-Andre Fleury

Marc-Andre Fleury is a Canadian net minder who has spent 15 years in the NHL with the Pittsburgh Penguins and the Vegas Golden Knights. Fleury was drafted first overall in the 2004 2003 NHL draft, one of only three goalies to ever be taken with the first pick. Fleury has been to the playoffs 12 times now, winning the Stanley Cup with the Penguins in 2009, 2016 and 2017. Fleury was also able to take the Vegas Golden Knights all the way to the Stanley Cup Finals in their inaugural season.

Henrik Lundqvist

Henrik Lundqvist is a Swedish NHL goaltender who has spent his entire 13 year career with the New York Rangers. Lundqvist led the rangers to the playoffs 11-of-13 years of his career. He also led the Rangers to the Stanley Cup Finals in 2014. Lundqvist is a two-time All Star and the recipient of the 2012 Vezina best goalie award.
